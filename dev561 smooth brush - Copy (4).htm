What is Smooth Brush

Smooth brush is a quick guide on how to paint, it gives simple and quick answers to common question related to painting, it gives straightforward simple anwers to day to day painting inquiries. It is a one stop shop on how to paint.

If you are new to painting, this page is an excellent resource, it will teach you the basics of painting, it will provide you an insight on what painting tools to use, and techniques in painting.

It contains videos made by experts, that teaches surface preparations, painting techniques, and paint tool maintenance (a complete paint cycle), plus there are other how-to info. That will assist you in your future painting projects.

Commonly asked question and answers

I would like to paint my room, what do i need to do ?

Good question,If you go to a paint store, a paint associate would ask you some typical question to help you in your project, here are some common ones to prepare you.

What are you painting, is it a bedroom, kitchen, office space, garage, garage door, basement walls, etc. ?
Tell the associate what you are painting.

Do you have a color in my mind?
It will be easier if you know in advance what exact color you want, you can pick-out a color swatch from the color kiosk, or any paint brochure, the swatches and brochures are normally free, and you can take them home, to compare against your walls to see how it looks like. if you are ready, bring to the store you color selection with the approriate color- code, and the paint department will dispense the color you requested. there are cases you may require a color match, some paint stores can color match by using a computerized scanner.

Do i want my paint to be shinny or no shine (this is called sheen)?
The different type of sheen is as follows:

  1. Flat - No shine, normally use on ceilings.
  2. Eggshell - Has little shine, easy to wash, normally use on walls and all-living areas.
  3. Satin - Has a higher shine, easy to wash, normally use on Kitchen and Bathrooms, Kids room.
  4. Semi-Gloss - Shinny, very easy to wash, normally use on trims, doors, hi-traffic areas.
  5. High-Gloss - Very Shinny, very easy to wash, normally use on high-traffic areas and trims, Normally you will need to apply a primer for this type of paint.

    6. Tip: the shinner the paint, the easier to clean.

How do i repair a crack on my dry wall ?

To repair the drywall, you need to
  1. Clean the damage surface.
  2. Apply a repair dry wall putty (Use a putty knife to spread the putty).
  3. When the putty is dry, use a sandpaper to smoothen the repaired area.

    4. Tip: use a 120 grit for smoothing.

  4. Clean the smoothened repaired area.
  5. You now have the option to paint.

Do i need to apply primer or can i apply paint and primer in one ?

Traditionally primer is applied first then after the primer has dried you may proceed to apply the paint, today there are paints that are classified as paint and primer in one, you do not need to apply a seperate primer, you may apply this type of paint to save you time and money. (Refer to Primer section for additional details)

How do I apply the paint ?

  1. Before applying the paint, make sure that the surface is clean, and all cracks and imperfections have been fixed.
  2. Prepare the following tools, to be used in painting, and they are; A paint brush, Painters tape, Paint tray, drop sheets, a can of paint and paint rollers with roller sleeves.
  3. Cover the edges of walls and trims you wish not to be painted using a Painters tape.
  4. Open the can of paint, and pour the paint into the paint tray.
  5. Use the brush and dip it to the painter tray, remove excess paint from the brush by scraping againts the paint tray.
  6. Paint the corners of the wall first, paint approximately 2 icnhes from the painters tape.
  7. Dip the paint roller into the paint tray, and scrape of excess paint using the grooves in the paint tray.
  8. Wait for about 5 seconds before removing the paint roller from the paint tray, this is done to prevent any spillage.
  9. Customarily you make the "W" technique, basically the paint is applied in a wide "W" pattern, then paint veritically to cover the gaps bewrteen the "W".
  10. Work in 3 foot sections.
  11. Frequently dip the roller into the paint tray, do not let the paint roller to dry.
  12. If you wish to apply a second coat, wait for the first layer to dry for an hour, then apply the second coat.
  13. After painting, let the paint to dry, and then carefully remove the tape.

Surface Preparation

Previously painted surface

  1. Fixed any cracks by applying a putty then smoothen the surface using a sand paper.
  2. Wipe off the dust or any dirt.
  3. if the surface is glossy or oily, you may apply a primer. this will make the paint adhere to the surface.

Painting over wall-paper

  1. If applying paint over wall-paper, apply first a oil-based primer.

Painting over new drywall

  1. Clean the surface of the drywall.
  2. Smoothen any drywall mad.
  3. Apply a primer for drywall.

Type of Paints

Latex paint

  1. Water is used as the solvent.
  2. The paint dries quickly, usually an hour.
  3. Some type of paints is 100% acrylic, meaning it has more glue, and has less water content.

Oil based paint

  1. Oil is used as the solvent.
  2. The paint dries longer, usually more than an hour.

Painting tools

Painters tape

  1. Use paint tape to block off areas to be painted and reduce the need to repaint over areas splotched with the wrong colour.
  2. Use the delicate type painters tape to cover sensitive areas.
  3. Use the All-surface type painters tape to cover multiple surface types.

Paint brush

  1. Use between 2 to 2 1/2 inch. size brushes if you are new to painting.
  2. Use a cutting brush to paint corners.
  3. Use a 3 inch brush to paint wider areas.

Painters tray and tray liner

  1. It is a container used to pour paint when painting.
  2. It comes in metal or plastic type.
  3. The tray liner is used to be place on top of the paint tray.
  4. The tray liner is used to pour paint when painting, after painting throw away the paint liner.

Paint roller sleeves

  1. A paint roller is used to spread the paint over a surface, the surface could be a drywall, stucco, concrete wall or wooden surface.
  2. There are many types of paint roller, most commonly used is a 10mm nap paint roller, the rule is the thicker the nap the more paint is aborb and ther is less drag when spreading the paint on the surface.
  3. There is a microfiber paint rollers, this gives a special coating or lives a fine smooth finish when painting.
  4. Buy a dependable paint roller, usually the industrial type, this are meant to last.

Drop sheets

  1. Use drop sheets to cover surfaces that you protected from paint spillage.
  2. Drop sheets comes in different sizes, so be sure that you are buying that is enough to cover the areas you want to protected.
  3. There paper type drop sheets and plastic type drop sheets.

Dry wall repair kits

  1. If you have cracks on your drywall, use a putty to cover the cracks, now depending on the size of cracks you may have in your wall you may need additional tools to cover up the crack or hole, if the size of the hole is bigger than 4 icnhes it wpuld be wise to use a mesh, to cover the hall then apply the putty.
  2. Putty comes in different types, there is a putty that is pink in color when applied then turns into white when it is dry. There are putty that is light in nature and dry quickly.

Wood filler

  1. There are 2 types of wood filler, one use use Internal use and one for external, we will be focusing more on internal wood fillers, the main function of wood fillers is to fill up cracks found on the wood surface.
  2. Use a spatula to apply the wood filler, make sure you fill up the inside of the crack.
  3. Let the wood filler dry completey or cure, then you make proceed to smoothen the cured area.
  4. There are wood fillers that are paintable and stainable.

Paint extension pole

  1. A paint extension pole is used to paint hard to reach areas.
  2. They usually comes in differnet types, there are wood, plastic and metal types.
  3. They come in different sizes, 4 ft. to 6 ft, that extends to 8 ft to 20 ft.
  4. The paint roller is screwed to the end of the paint extension pole.

Paint solvent

  1. A paint solvent is used to dilute paint from surfaces, or brush.
  2. They come in various forms, like paint thinner, varsol, mythalyn hydrate.
  3. Brushes are usually soak in the solvent to dilute the paint attach to the brush bristles, after which you could rinse the brush under a warm runningn water or you can wipe it off using a rag.
  4. If the paint you are using is a latex based paint, the solvent you will use is water to dilute the paint.

Primer

Primer qualities

  1. A primer seals any minor cracks on the surface.
  2. A primer makes the paint adheres to the surface.
  3. A primer hides stains found on the surface.
  4. A primer eliminates odors radiating on the surface.
